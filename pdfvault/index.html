Features Built for Privacy

Scan Beautifully

Point your camera and PDFVault finds the page edges, captures automatically when steady, and cleans up shadows and bleed-through. Two-sided ID card mode combines front and back onto one page.

Keep It Private

Documents you mark as private are stored in a separate area of the app, hidden from the system Files app and protected by Face ID, Touch ID, or your device passcode. You can also add a password to individual PDFs.

Redact with Confidence

PDFVault helps you black out sensitive information before sharing. It suggests items to redact (like ID numbers or emails) — you review, adjust, and apply. Redaction removes the text underneath the box, not just the visual mark.

A Brain That Works Offline

Optional on-device AI reads your scan and suggests a clean filename, a folder, and a three-bullet summary. Ask questions about your document and search by meaning ("car trouble" finds the mechanic's invoice).

Everyday Toolbox

Compress PDFs and photos for email. Convert PDF to JPG and Word to PDF. Sign documents with your finger and strip hidden metadata (location, device info) before sharing.

About the one-time AI download

Everything PDFVault does with your documents — scanning, text recognition, redaction, search, summaries — runs on your device. The app doesn't upload your files, doesn't require an account, and works in airplane mode.

The optional Privacy Brain features use small AI models that are downloaded once from Hugging Face, a public model-hosting service. There are up to three downloads: a language model (~350MB) for naming, summaries, and chat; a vision add-on (~250MB) for reading page images; and a compact embedding model (~35MB) for natural-language search. Only the model files travel over the network — your documents and scans are never part of any download or upload. After the download, all AI features run fully offline. AI features are optional: you can delete the models anytime in Settings, and the app works without them.
